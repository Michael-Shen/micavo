EatOrNot AI

EatOrNot AI helps people decide whether a meal fits their day. It supports photo and voice food input, estimates calories and macros, tracks daily nutrition progress, and uses authorized Apple Health context for personalized coaching.

  • Photo and voice meal analysis with clear eat-or-adjust guidance.
  • Daily calorie and macro tracking designed for quick decisions.
  • Pro and Premium coaching for weekly insights and habit patterns.

Outshine

Outshine is an AI Career Copilot for meetings: live coaching cards that help users ask sharper questions and know what to say next, plus an after-meeting summary.

  • Live coaching cards: a strategic question, a career signal, and what to say next.
  • One-time free 60-minute credit, then Pro (400 min/month) or Premium (800 min/month, cross-meeting AI memory, post-meeting AI conversation).
  • Automatic meeting summaries with decisions, action items, open questions, and risks.

Productivity system

Critical Choice

Critical Choice brings the Eisenhower Matrix into a modern task manager. It helps users sort work by urgency and importance, focus on high-leverage tasks, sync calendar events, and collaborate through cloud projects.

  • Visual quadrant organization for urgent and important work.
  • Daily task view, achievements, calendar sync, and cloud sync.
  • Premium features for teams, custom tags, assignments, and collaboration.

Approach

Small products, serious product thinking.

Micavo favors narrow, useful products over broad AI demos. The goal is software that feels calm, actionable, and trustworthy in daily life.

01

Decisions, not dashboards

EatOrNot never shows a calorie dashboard. Every meal gets one verdict: eat it, or adjust it.

02

Context, not guesswork

Critical Choice syncs your calendar so the quadrant view reflects your actual day — not a task list you have to update by hand every time your schedule changes.

03

Action, not novelty

Outshine doesn't wait for the meeting to end. Mid-call, it hands you one sharp question, one risk, and one line to say next — not a dashboard.
